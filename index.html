Corroborated Findings (both sources agree)

Basic Identity

  • Vladimir Viktorovich Morozov, born 16 June 1992 in Novosibirsk, Russia.
  • Russian competitive swimmer, retired.
  • Sprint specialist — versatile across freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly, and individual medley.
  • Swam collegiately at the University of Southern California (USC); became an All-American.
  • Competed internationally for Russia, and later as a neutral athlete (ROC / Russian Swimming Federation) due to doping-related sanctions on Russia.
  • Retired from professional swimming, announced in 2024.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Olympic Career

  • 2012 London (Russia): Bronze medal — 4×100m freestyle relay. Also competed in 100m backstroke and 4×100m medley relay.
  • 2016 Rio (Russia): Competed in 50m freestyle, 100m freestyle, 4×100m freestyle relay, 4×100m medley relay. Did not medal.
  • 2020 Tokyo (ROC): Competed in 50m freestyle and 4×100m freestyle relay. Did not medal.
  • Summary: 0 gold, 0 silver, 1 bronze across three Olympics.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

World Championships (Short Course)

  • Dominant in short-course (25m) World Championships across multiple editions beginning with 2012 Istanbul, where he won multiple medals including gold in relays.
  • One of the most decorated short-course swimmers of his era.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

FINA Swimming World Cup

  • One of the most successful swimmers ever on the short-course World Cup circuit.
  • Well over 100 gold medals (Perplexity: "well over 100"; Grok: 123 gold out of 176 total medals).
  • Set and tied multiple records in 100m IM and 50m freestyle.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

100m Individual Medley — World Record

  • Set a world record in the short-course 100m individual medley at 50.26 seconds (2018 World Cup). European record, former WR.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

European Championships (Short Course)

  • Extremely successful at European Short Course Championships, including:
  • 2012 Chartres: Gold in 100m freestyle and 100m IM; silver in 50m freestyle; bronze in 50m backstroke.
  • 2017 Copenhagen: Broke Russian national record in 50m freestyle twice; won gold.
  • 2019 Glasgow: Won seven gold medals, including 50m freestyle and 50m breaststroke (European record).
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Career-Wide Summary

  • One of the most versatile sprint swimmers of his generation.
  • Exceptional short-course swimmer; strong relay anchor.
  • Career shaped and ultimately limited by Russian doping sanctions — competed as ROC/neutral athlete in later years, missed competitions due to national federation bans, and retired earlier than planned due to circumstances beyond his control.
  • Post-retirement, involved in coaching and swimming instruction.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Long Course World Championships Detail

  • 2013 Barcelona: Silver 50m free (21.47, Russian record).
  • 2015 Kazan, 2017 Budapest, 2019 Gwangju: Multiple silver/bronze and high placements in relays and 50m events. Contributed to Russian relay records.

Short Course World Championships Detail

  • Golds in 50m and 100m freestyle at 2012 and 2018 editions. Multiple relay golds across 2012 Istanbul, 2014 Doha, 2016 Windsor, 2018 Hangzhou, and 2021 Abu Dhabi. Set or contributed to multiple relay records.

Universiade

NCAA

  • Multi-time NCAA champion (2013) at USC. First swimmer to break 18 seconds for a 50-yard freestyle relay split (17.86).
